Technical Design & Stack — judge handout
Browser-based real-time speech coach
Built on Google, top to bottom · QuackHacks Google Track

Listens to a rehearsal, gives a light live nudge — on screen or spoken aloud — then returns a context-aware report judged against the speaker's own slides & audience — including where their tone contradicts their words. One capture, two engines.

Two paths over one capture

One mic capture Web Audio API · MediaRecorder · React+Vite SPA
Live · on-device Real-time nudge
volumepitch pacepauses → rule engine → one calm nudge
pace > 2.2 syll/s pitch var < 12 Hz vol < −45 dBFS silence > 2 s
On stop · cloud Context report + tone
clip → Cloud Run → Speech-to-Text v2 Gemini ×3 → report
Gemini judges meaning; the math judges delivery. The report reads every metric through your slides + audience (pace, density, coverage). Tone flags where prosody is the opposite of the words — the verdict lives in the disagreement.

Hands-free coaching — built for your ears, so your eyes stay up

Web Speech browser text-to-speech · opt-in
condition holds 3 s → speaks one short cue → 10 s cooldown
A deliberately higher bar than the on-screen nudge — speech interrupts, so it waits longer and rests longer, and never nags. Pace & volume cues like "ease off the pace" play aloud through speakers or earbuds, so you can rehearse with your eyes on the room instead of the screen.
